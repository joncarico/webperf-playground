2023 Toyota RAV4 Adventure Vs. 2023 Subaru Forester Wilderness

By Amrit Balraj
Published 16 minutes ago

Donning a more rugged avatar, here's why the Forester Wilderness and Rav4 Adventure are far more competent than your typical compact crossover SUV

Subaru-Forester_Wilderness

The Forester Wilderness and the RAV4 Adventure are two compact SUVs that have been beefed up for off-road adventures. Some of their rivals include the Mazda CX-50, and the Ford Bronco Sport. The RAV4 is one of Toyota’s most successful models, in fact, in 2022 it was the best-selling compact SUV in the U.S. and the best-selling non-pickup car. It is fair to say it has been the people’s choice for its affordability, comfortable interiors, and luggage space. The Subaru Forester Wilderness is a relatively new trim, it was first launched in 2022.

The RAV4 Adventure differentiates itself from the XLE trim with standard AWD, bigger 19-inch wheels compared to 17-inch, and a more aggressive appearance. The Forester Wilderness trim has more to offer when compared to other Forester trims, specifically half an inch of additional ground clearance, double the towing capacity with 3,000 pounds, and a more rugged exterior appearance package. So how do these two SUVs compare with each other? Let’s take a closer look at what these SUVs offer.

How Do The Rav4 And Forester Compare On Pavement?

Performance-wise, the Subaru Wilderness comes with a 2.4-Liter flat-four BOXER engine, that produces 182 horsepower at 5800 RPM and 176 pound-feet of torque at 4400 RPM. This engine is paired with a CVT transmission with manual mode for better off-road control. The Wilderness also features Subaru’s Symmetrical AWD, which balances power delivery to each wheel to improve traction in slippery or slushy terrain.

The RAV4 Adventure comes with a similar 2.5-Liter inline-four outputting 203 horsepower at 6,600 RPM and 184 pound-feet of torque at 5,500 RPM. This is mated to an eight-Speed automatic gearbox with an intelligent AWD, having the ability to disconnect the rear axle when it is not required.

On paper, the RAV4 is faster than the Forester, however, the latter has the advantage of its WRX heritage with its handling, making it more flickable on sharp turns, but expect body roll on both. On the tarmac, the RAV4’s insulated interiors are better at cutting down on road noise when compared to the Forester. As per the EPA, The Forester Wilderness returns a highway mileage of 25 MPG and 28 MPG in the city for a combined gas mileage of 26 MPG. The RAV4 Adventure gives you more for your money with a gas mileage of 27 MPG in the city, 35 MPG on the highway, and combined mileage of 30 MPG,

How Capable Are They Off-Road?

Front 3/4 shot of a 2021 Toyota RAV4

While both of these cars have several off-road focussed features, which one of these has the edge over the other? The Forester Wilderness comes with 9.2 inches of ground clearance with a protective metal skid plate, making it moderately taller than the RAV4 at 8.6 inches. One unique feature of the Wilderness is a front-facing 180° trail camera, which makes it easier to get a read on your surroundings while off-roading. The Subaru also has redesigned front bumpers and rear fenders to give it a steeper approach and departure angles when compared to the Toyota.

The Wilderness has the X-Mode which comes standard on the Outback, which works automatically to shift between Snow/Dirt and Deep Snow/Mud mode depending on tire traction. It allows AWD to channel power to the right tires if there is slippage during sticky situations. The X-Mode also works in controlling hill descent, allowing the driver to focus on steering while the system takes care of acceleration and braking. When X-Mode is active, the transmission also remains at a lower gear to increase available power and torque to the driver.

A front 3/4 shot of a Subaru Forester Wilderness in the woods

The RAV4 similarly gets an adaptive AWD system with torque vectoring and Multi-Terrain Select System which can be activated for Snow, Dirt, or Mud/Sand via a center console knob. The system can distribute 50% of the available torque to the rear or left/right for improving traction based on the terrain. However, the Subaru Wilderness has the better set of tires - the Yokohama Geolander A/T GO15, which are sturdy off-road and performs well on the road as well. Another cool feature is that the Wilderness comes with a full-size spare tire, which should come in hand if you ever have a flat when you're out and about on the trails. The Toyota comes with Falken Wildpeak A/T Trail 01A, which is also useful off-road.

We already know that the Subaru Wilderness has the highest towing capacity among all of its variants at 3,000 pounds. This is due to the significant final drive ratio of 4.11:1 of its specially tuned gearbox, which is distinctive to the Wilderness variant. The RAV4 takes the cake with a higher towing capacity of 3,500 pounds, which means it can be trusted to haul more gear. Meanwhile, the Forester Wilderness has a sturdier roof rack with 220 pounds of dynamic load capacity and 800 pounds of static capacity, making it ideal for roof-mounted overlanding tents.

Which SUV Leads in Interior Comfort and Space?

A shot of the dashboard in the 2024 Subaru Crosstrek Wilderness

The Wilderness comes with StarTex water-repellant upholstery which is easy to clean if they get muddy while being comfortable and breathable at the same time. While it appears to be similar to leather, it is actuallysynthetic polyurethane. There is more than adequate headroom and shoulder space for five adults, and tons of storage options.

There is an 8.0-inch infotainment screen loaded with Subaru’s proprietary Starlink interface which controls smart device connectivity, navigation, and a host of safety features like Automatic Collision Notification and Enhanced Roadside Assistance. The RAV4 also comes with comfortable interiors and spacious interiors with additional options like heated and ventilated seats, rain-sensing wipers, and a heated leather steering wheel. The RAV4 also has an 8.0 infotainment screen, wireless charging, and nearby object detection with sonar sensors.

A shot of the dashboard in a Toyota Rav4 SE Hybrid

Now we come to the bottom line, the RAV-4 Adventure has an MSRP of $35,805 with a host of options that can push the price north of $40,000 and the Subaru Wilderness starts at $35,545, which also comes with its own set of optional extras. Of the two models, if its better off-road performance you seek, you're better off with the old-school Forester. It packs a more robust all-wheel drive system with better gear. But if you're going to spend most of your time on the road and want an crossover that is more capable than your standard Rav4, the Adventure trim can certain do that. It is a far more modern vehicle with better tech and a refined cabin. Hopefully, we have helped you narrow your choices when it comes to which one of these excellent crossover SUVs is right for you.
